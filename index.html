So looking at the line chart of world wide gross and also looking at the area chart of domestic gross we can see that two generes have exploded in popularity internationally and stateside since the 1990s to the present, with those two genres being Action movies, and Adventure movies. Alright so lets look deeper into
how action and adventure generes do in other measurements of success in regards to movies. Let's look at DVD sales and Blu-ray sales next.


When examining DVD sales, we can see that Adventure movies dominated the market, generating approximately $13.35 billion domestically — far surpassing other genres. Action movies followed in second place with around $8.1 billion. On the Blu-ray side, the competition was much closer: Action films brought in about $5.8 billion, while Adventure movies earned roughly $5.45 billion. Both genres significantly outperformed all others, with the next highest, Drama, trailing at $1.6 billion.


Let us now turn our attention to production budgets and how they are related to the gross(amount of money) a movie makes in theaters.

By toggling the filter and examining specific genres, we can see a strong positive correlation between production budget and both opening weekend gross and total worldwide gross for the Action, Adventure, and Drama genres. In contrast, genres like Horror, Concert, and Reality show much weaker correlations. This data suggests that low-budget or independent studios may have a better chance of succeeding by producing films in the Horror, Concert, or Reality genres, while major studios are better positioned to invest heavily in Action, Adventure, and Drama films to pursue larger returns


Now, let's move on to examining movie legs by genre. In this context, "legs" refers to a film's ability to exceed its projected run by maintaining audience interest and generating box office revenue beyond its opening weekend.

In a surprising twist, Drama and Comedy films appear to have significantly longer legs compared to other genres. This suggests that, even if their opening weekends aren't as strong, these genres tend to sustain audience interest and box office performance over a longer period of time.
